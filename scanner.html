How to scan well

1. Use a plain background that contrasts with the object (e.g., white wall / dark object).
2. Keep the object centred and roughly the same size in every shot.
3. Hold the phone upright (portrait) and level with the object's middle.
4. Take 12–24 shots evenly spaced through a full circle.
5. In Rotate object mode, keep the phone still and turn the object one step per shot.

This scanner reconstructs the object's visual hull from its outlines — bowls and deep hollows facing the camera cannot be captured, and fine detail is approximate. It is best suited to compact, convex-ish objects.

What happens here

Each photo's silhouette is projected through a voxel grid from its recorded angle; space outside any silhouette is carved away (visual-hull reconstruction). The remaining volume is converted to a triangle mesh with a surface-nets algorithm. Everything runs on your phone — no upload.

Printing notes

The STL is binary, watertight, oriented Z-up with the base on the build plate, and sized in millimetres — ready for Cura, PrusaSlicer, Bambu Studio or any other slicer. If your slicer reports issues, its built-in repair (or an online repair service) will resolve them in seconds.
